What happened to The Great Suspender?

The Great Suspender was one of the most popular Chrome extensions ever — over 2 million users relied on it to free up memory by suspending inactive tabs. Then, in 2021, the original developer sold the extension to an unknown party, who quietly pushed an update containing malware that tracked users and executed remote code.

Google removed it from the Chrome Web Store and force-disabled it on every browser. Overnight, millions of suspended tabs became unrecoverable. The forks that followed inherited the same trust problem: who really controls the code, and what happens at the next sale?

Tab Suspender was built to answer that question differently — no tracking, no analytics, no data collection, ever. Just a reliable tool that does one job well.
